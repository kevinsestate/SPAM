# SPAM Assembly Instructions
### Useful links
- Capstan original designs: https://github.com/aaedmusa/Capstan-Drive/tree/main 
- Capstan explenation video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MwIBTbumd1Q&pp=ygUNY2Fwc3RhbiBkcml2ZQ%3D%3D 
---
### Important notes
1. Whatever you do, do not slide any of the sliding components off of their respective rails. The sliders have loose ball bearings in them and it is not pretty
when they fall out
2. Much of the hardware required to assemble SPAM are in their respective places, otherwise it should be found in misc. bags 
3. Before assembling the base you should install the last couple nuts into the bottom of the base. They match with the large philips head screws
--- 
### Components
| Component Name | Quantity Required |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Static Arm: the arms with the linear encoders attatched | 2 |
| Base Plate | 1 |
| Rotating Arm: the arm without the linear encoders attatched (there is a good reason) | 1 |
| Base + Capstan (pronounced kap-stn) assembly: this is what will rotate the arm, it has motors for arm and MUT rotation | 1 |
| Linear Encoders: 4 of these are already on the static arms, the remaining will end up on the rotating arm | 6 |
---
### Overall Workflow
Base(1) --> Plate(2) --> Static Arms(3) --> Rotating Arms(4) --> Linear Encoders(4) --> Sleds(3 & 4)
---
### Base --> Plate
The base --> plate attatchment is the first thing you should tackle
Steps:
1. Align the mounting holes in the base with the plate
2. Attach with 4xM5 screws
3. Done
---
### Static Arms
Static arms --> base is quite simple as well
Steps:
1. Slide the arms onto the base by aligning the metal rods with the holes in the arms
2. One of the arms will likely give you trouble on step 1. I promise they will go oon with a little bit of elbow grease
3. Once the arms are on, you can secure them with the large philips head screws
4. Done
---
### Rotating Arm
Rotating arm --> capstan large drum, this one is a bit more involved 
Steps:
1. Remove any screws that may be in the curved slider
2. Do your best to align the arm, capstan drum, and slider and slide the arm onto the metal rods just like the previous arms
3. Fix the arm to the slider using large screws (the belts may be annoying for this)
4. Fix the arm to the Capstan using an M5 screw
5. Insert the belt gears to the Capstan, ensuring the belt is wrapped around the gears before attaching with an M4 screw
6. This would be a good time to attach the green flags that actuate the optical limit switches
7. Done
---
### Linear Encoders 
Linear encoders --> Rotating arm, this is quite simple
Steps:
1. Align the encoders with the arm
2. Attach with M4 screws on both ends
3. Done
---
### Sleds
Sleds + Mounts + Linear Encoders
Steps:
1. Align the sleds with their respective linear encoders and fix with M5 screws 
2. Find the proper mount for the sled you are working on, and attach with M5 screws
3. Repeat steps 1 and 2 for all mounts + sleds as required
4. Done
---
### Lens and Horns
This is easy!
1. Horns connect with M4 screws
2. Lenses slot into place on their mounts
